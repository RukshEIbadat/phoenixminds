CBT — Cognitive Behavioural Therapy
Thought-Feeling-Behaviour Triangle
Help your child identify the connection between thoughts, emotions, and actions. Suitable for verbal children aged 5 and above.
  • Draw a triangle together — label each corner: "What I think / What I feel / What I do"
  • Use a recent situation: "When the toy was taken, what did you think?"
  • Help child name the feeling using an emotion chart — do not guess for them
  • Explore what they did — and together think of 3 other things they could do
  • Practice the new thought/action using role-play for 5 minutes
DBT — Dialectical Behaviour Therapy
TIPP Skills — Emotional Regulation
TIPP (Temperature, Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Progressive relaxation) for managing overwhelming emotions quickly.
  • T — Cold water on face or ice pack on wrists during meltdown onset (30 seconds)
  • I — 2 minutes of jumping jacks or star jumps to discharge body tension
  • P — Breathe in for 4 counts, hold 2, out for 6 — repeat 5 times together
  • P — Squeeze and release each muscle group: hands, arms, shoulders, legs, feet
  • After regulation: use a feelings card to name what happened — do not debrief during crisis
ABA — Applied Behaviour Analysis
Discrete Trial Training at Home
Structured teaching in short, repeatable trials to build skills. Parent version — no professional certification required for home use.
  • Choose ONE skill: e.g., "Touch your nose when asked"
  • Give clear instruction: "Touch your nose" — wait 3 seconds
  • If correct: immediate praise + preferred item reward
  • If incorrect: physically guide the movement — then reward the guided response
  • 10 trials per session, twice daily, 5 days per week — log each trial on the data sheet
Social Stories
Personalised Social Narrative
ZETA-9 generates personalised social stories based on your child's specific challenges. Read together daily — 3 to 5 minutes before the anticipated situation.
  • Select the target scenario from the list (e.g., "Going to the doctor")
  • ZETA-9 generates a custom story using your child's name, preferred characters, and photo if uploaded
  • Read slowly — 1 page per minute. Let child hold the book or screen
  • After reading: ask 2 questions about what the character did — do not quiz, explore
  • Repeat the same story for 5 days before introducing the real situation
Play Therapy
Child-Directed Play — 30 Minutes Daily
Filial therapy approach — you follow the child's lead. No questions, no corrections, no directions. Build connection and regulation capacity.
  • Let the child choose the activity entirely — no redirecting
  • Describe what you see: "You are putting the red block on top"
  • Reflect feelings: "You seem really happy when that tower falls"
  • Set only safety limits — everything else is permitted in play
  • No phone, no interruptions — 30 full minutes of undivided presence
Mindfulness for Parents
Caregiver Regulation First
A dysregulated parent cannot regulate a dysregulated child. This module supports the primary caregiver's own mental health as a therapeutic intervention.

  • Self-compassion pause: place hand on chest and say "This is hard. I am doing my best."
  • Identify your personal triggers — log them in the parent mental health section weekly
  • Take 3 minutes alone after a crisis before re-engaging — permission granted
